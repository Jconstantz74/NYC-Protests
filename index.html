Brooklyn saw a Summer of Protests. Here's What Happened.

By Jo Constantz Oct. 14, 2020

Protests have gripped the nation since George Floyd was killed by police in May. The Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project (ACLED), collected data on the protests for its U.S. Crisis Monitor, a join project with the Bridging Divides Initiative (BDI) at Princeton University. In a report analyzing the data, ACLED noted that while 93% of the BLM protests across the country are peaceful, there has been an increase in both police intervention and the action of non-state groups, like militias and white supremacist groups.

Here's a look at what happened this summer in Brooklyn.

Type of protest
Peaceful protest
Violent demonstration
May 29, 2020: Thousands of protesters, together with State Senator Zellnor Myrie from Brooklyn, demonstrated at the Barclays Center. The senator was pepper sprayed and handcuffed by police. A woman was pushed to the ground by an officer. Some rioters vandalized NYPD cars, including setting vehicles on fire. Multiple officers were injured.
May 30, 2020: Thousands staged a protest over the death of George Floyd. Two NYPD vehicles drove into a crowd, hitting at least half a dozen people. The first vehicle accelerated and rammed through people standing in front of it. Demonstrators hurled bottles and debris at the police and attempted to throw a homemade firebomb into a police car with four officers inside.
May 31, 2020: Hundreds of BLM demonstrators gathered in Brooklyn to protest the death of George Floyd.
June 1 2020: Thousands of BLM demonstrators marched through Brooklyn to protest the death of George Floyd. Several community leaders, clergy members, and NYPD officers stood and knelt outside the NYPD's 88th Precinct to show their unity with the demonstrators.
June 2, 2020: People vandalized the window of Black Nile Restaurant in the Crown Heights neighorhood during protests in support of the BLM movement.
June 2, 2020: Thousands of protesters supporting the BLM movement marched through Downtown Brooklyn, past Borough Hall, and then returned to the Barclays Center. Protesters called George Floyd's name and the names of other people of color killed by the police around the country.
June 3, 2020: Thousands of protesters assembled at Cadman Plaza in Downtown Brooklyn to protest against police brutality and the death of George Floyd. Police used batons and pepper spray to disperse protesters who remained after curfew.
June 4, 2020: Between 500 and 600 BLM demonstrators gathered on Flatbush Avenue in Brooklyn to protest the death of George Floyd.
June 5, 2020: People staged several protests in Brooklyn over the death of George Floyd. Police forces intervened and arrested dozens when the protest extended past curfew.
June 6, 2020: About 15,000 people held a protest in East Flatbush in support of the BLM movement and in the name of George Floyd. The event was called "Long Walk to Freedom," and a former state senator participated.
June 7, 2020: Thousands of protesters staged a dozen protests in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality and the death of George Floyd.
June 8, 2020: Thousands of protesters staged six protests in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality and the death of George Floyd.
June 9, 2020: About 2,000 people staged a protest in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality.
June 10, 2020: Thousands of protesters staged four protests in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality and the death of George Floyd.
June 11, 2020: Protesters staged two protests in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality and the death of George Floyd.
June 12, 2020: Thousands of protesters staged 11 protests in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality and the death of George Floyd.
June 13, 2020: Hundreds of protesters staged two protests in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality and the death of George Floyd.
June 14, 2020: Several thousand people staged protests in Brooklyn, including one in Bushwick, in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality.
June 14, 2020: Thousands of protesters staged 15 protests in Brooklyn in support of the Black Trans Lives Matter movement, and against police brutality and the death of George Floyd
June 16, 2020: Protesters staged two protests in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality and the death of George Floyd.
June 17, 2020: Protesters staged four protests in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality and the death of George Floyd.
June 18, 2020: Protesters staged six protests in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality and the death of George Floyd.
June 19, 2020: Thousands of people, including LGBT activists, staged a Juneteenth protest in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality.
June 20, 2020: Protesters staged six protests in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality and the death of George Floyd.
June 22, 2020: Protesters, including a group of African-American women, marched to Brooklyn Borough Hall in Brooklyn to support the Black Women Lives Matter/ BLM movement, and to condemn police brutality following the deaths of George Floyd and Breonna Taylor.
June 26, 2020: Protesters staged two BLM protests in Brooklyn in the wake of the deaths of George Floyd and Breonna Taylor. Several African-American women protested in Prospect and McCarren Parks in support of the Black Women's Lives Matter/ BLM movement.
July 4, 2020: People gathered for a march called "Confronting July 4th" in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality following the death of George Floyd.
July 17, 2020: About two dozen protesters marched from Bed-Stuy to Washington Square Park in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality following George Floyd's death.
July 25, 2020: People marched in a protest from Cadman Plaza Park across the Brooklyn Bridge in support of the BLM movement and against police brutality in the wake of George Floyd's death.
August 23, 2020: People gathered in Brooklyn to honor the life of Yusuf Hawkins, a Black teenager who was fatally shot by white youths in 1989. The protest also showed support for the Black Lives Matter movement and against the death of George Floyd and the shooting of Jacob Blake.
September 11, 2020: Hundreds of people staged a protest in Brooklyn in support of the BLM movement. They dedicated the protest to a fellow activist who was killed in a bicycle crash earlier that week.
September 23, 2020: More than 600 people attended a BLM protest outside the Barclays Center against the Kentucky grand jury's decision not to charge three Louisville police officers in the death of Breonna Taylor.
September 24, 2020: Hundreds held a protest in Bushwick over the latest developments in the Breonna Taylor case and in support of the BLM movement.
September 25, 2020: Peaceful protests occurred in Brooklyn over the latest developments in the Breonna Taylor case and in support of the BLM movement. At one point, protesters sat down on the Brooklyn Bridge, temporarily obstructing traffic.
